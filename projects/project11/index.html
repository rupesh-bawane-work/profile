G20 Sovereign CDS Connectedness Analysis

TVP-VAR Modeling • Volatility Spillovers • Risk Determinant Analysis

Business Problem

Financial contagion among G20 nations can lead to systemic collapses. Traditional risk models often fail to capture the dynamic, time-varying nature of how sovereign credit risk spreads. The challenge is to quantify these spillovers and identify which countries act as the primary transmitters of financial instability during global crises.

Project Overview

This project implements a comprehensive TVP-VAR Extended Joint Connectedness framework to analyze sovereign CDS returns. It spans the entire analytical pipeline: from data stationarity testing and growth rate visualization to time-varying spillover estimation and robust regression analysis of risk determinants.

Analysis Lifecycle Definition

  • Stage 1: Data Transformation & Stationarity (Log Returns)
  • Stage 2: Descriptive Statistics & Unit Root Testing (Table 1)
  • Stage 3: TVP-VAR Connectedness Estimation (Table 2)
  • Stage 4: Dynamic Spillovers & Net Position Analysis (Figs 2, 3, 4)
  • Stage 5: Determinant Analysis (HAC & Quantile Regressions - Tables 4, 5)
  • Stage 6: Granular Directional Analysis (Appendix A1, A2)

Data Requirement

  • Sovereign CDS Daily Prices (G20 Nations)
  • Global Uncertainty Indices (VIX, GPR, TPU, CPU)
  • Economic Policy Uncertainty Indicators
  • Historical Crisis Timelines (COVID-19, etc.)
